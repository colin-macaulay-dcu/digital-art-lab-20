What This Resource Covers

Core Objectives

Across all four activities, students build the same core skills — applied to different contexts.

🎨

Creating

Design a digital or physical portrait incorporating line, colour, texture — to reflect personality, era, or artistic style.

🎤

Performing & Presenting

Animate the portrait using the Talkr app and record a voiceover script combining oral language, storytelling, and digital skills.

🔍

Responding & Connecting

Analyse and critique the character's context. Reflect on creative choices and engage in meaningful peer feedback.

Four Activities

Choose a Lesson

Each activity uses the same Talkr app workflow but applies it to a different theme.

1st – 2nd Class

Create a Character Profile: Roald Dahl's Willy Wonka

Bring a character from Charlie and the Chocolate Factory to life by creating a digital talking portrait.

Students explore the character of Willy Wonka by creating a visual and narrative character profile. They design a portrait incorporating his unique traits and whimsical personality, then bring it to life using the Talkr app to animate and record a voiceover.

Lesson Plan PDF↓ Download

Learning Outcomes

1

Creative Expression: Students produce a personalised portrait showcasing eccentric and imaginative traits.

2

Literary Understanding: Students demonstrate understanding of character and role in the story.

3

Digital Literacy: Students use animation and voice recording tools to bring their portrayal to life.

4

Communication Skills: Students craft and share a narrative voiceover reflecting the character's personality.

5

Reflective Learning: Students reflect on how artistic and narrative choices capture the character's essence.

3rd – 4th Class

Create an Artist Profile: The Movement of Fauvism

Using the Talkr app, create a digital character profile of a Fauvist artist.

Students explore bold Fauvism by creating a fictional artist. They design a portrait using exaggerated colours and simplified forms, then animate and record a voiceover expressing their character's artistic philosophy.

Lesson Plan PDF↓ Download

Learning Outcomes

1

Creative Expression: A vivid Fauvist-inspired portrait capturing the movement's style.

2

Art Historical Understanding: Knowledge of Fauvism — bold colours, simplified forms, emotional expression.

3

Digital Literacy: Using animation and voice recording to bring a Fauvist artist to life.

4

Communication Skills: Articulating the artist's backstory and philosophy through voiceover.

5

Reflective Learning: Reflecting on artistic choices and the emotional impact of Fauvist techniques.

3rd – 4th Class

Create a Titanic Passenger Profile

Create a digital talking portrait of an imaginary Titanic passenger.

Students create a fictional Titanic passenger combining historical context with creative storytelling. They design a digital portrait considering class, background, and motivations, then animate their character using the Talkr app.

Lesson Plan PDF↓ Download

Learning Outcomes

1

Creative Expression: A personalised Titanic character portrait reflecting the era.

2

Historical Understanding: Knowledge of the Titanic's social structure and significance.

3

Digital Literacy: Using digital drawing and animation tools.

4

Communication Skills: Crafting a backstory through recorded voiceover.

5

Reflective Learning: Connecting creative work to historical themes.

5th – 6th Class

Create a Viking Warrior Profile

Bring a Viking warrior character to life with a digital talking portrait.

Students create a Viking warrior, explorer, or craftsperson — combining historical understanding with artistic expression. They design and paint a portrait then bring the character to life with the Talkr app.

The Activity Workflow

Five Steps — Every Activity

The same five-step process works across all four activities.

01

Choose Your Character

Pick a character, historical figure, or artist. Consider their personality and background.

02

Design the Portrait

Draw or create a digital image — physical art materials, apps, or a combination.

03

Set Up the Avatar

Import your portrait into the Talkr app. Set up animation — eyes, mouth, facial movement.

04

Record the Voiceover

Write a short script and record it in character.

05

Share Your Work

Play your talking portrait. Engage in peer feedback, gallery walks, or a showcase.

Classroom Discussion

Key Thinking Questions

Higher-Order Thinking

  • Why do you think this character acts the way they do?
  • If your character could give advice today, what would they say?
  • How might your character's experiences have changed them?
  • What emotions did you try to show through your artistic choices?
  • How could art and technology help us understand historical figures?
  • If you could meet your character, what would you ask them?
  • How might their story differ if they lived today?

Lower-Order Thinking

  • Who is your character? What do they look like?
  • What is their name and where do they come from?
  • What do they do? What is their job or role?
  • What colours did you use and why?
  • What does your character say in their voiceover?
  • What materials did you use to create your portrait?
  • What did you enjoy most about this activity?

💬

Peer Feedback

Gallery walks, group rotations, or spotlight presentations for constructive feedback.

🔍

Reflect on artistic and narrative choices — what worked and what they'd change.

👁️

Focus on creativity, technique, and presentation — individual effort and progress.

What You'll Need

Resources & Materials

Art Materials

  • Paper: A3 or A4 drawing paper, sketchbooks, or cardstock
  • Sketching: Pencils, erasers, and sharpeners
  • Colour: Coloured pencils, markers, watercolours, acrylic paints
  • Chalk Pastels: Bright, bold colours — ideal for Fauvism
  • Collage: Coloured paper, scissors, glue sticks
  • Markers: Fine-tip black markers for outlines
  • Reference images: Visual examples for each theme

Digital Tools & Hardware

  • iPads or Tablets: One per student or per pair
  • Talkr App: Pre-installed and updated
  • Headphones with mics: For recording voiceovers
  • Styluses: For more precise digital drawing
  • Classroom Wi-Fi: Stable connection for app use
  • Drawing apps: Procreate, Tayasui Sketches, or Canva
  • Projector / Whiteboard: For demonstrations

Inclusive Teaching

Differentiation Strategies

All four activities are designed to be fully inclusive.

Content

Vary the Resources

Simplified summaries or video clips for support. In-depth texts for advanced learners.

Process

Scaffold the Steps

Character profile templates with sentence starters. Open-ended prompts for advanced learners.

Product

Flexible Output

Simple portrait and short script to detailed portrait with elaborate backstory. All valued equally.

Environment

Station-Based Support

Guided station, independent station, and tech help station for the app.

Assessment

Individual Progress

Assess effort, creativity, and engagement — not uniform standards.

Extension

Going Further

Design inventions (Wonka), philosophical mottos (Fauvism), dramatic Titanic storytelling, Viking symbolic patterns.
